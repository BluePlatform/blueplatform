Projects

Orient Finance PLC "Diriya Personal Loan" scheme

  • “Diriya” Loan Scheme introduce by Orient Finance Plc held on 28th of November 2014 at Bartleet Group Conference Hall for the members of The All Island School Children Transport Association.

  • The above loan scheme was very successful , since most of our members of the Association could utilized these funds against maintenance of their school transport vehicles. This has been a big relief for our members , specially during the school holidays.

  • We would take this opportunity to thank Mr. Nilantha Jayaneththi, A.G.M Marketing of the company spending all your valuable time, trusting us, introducing the above scheme regardless of the higher risk the company would face in re-settlement plans.However it is a great help for all of us.

safer school transport service with Orient Finance

  • As an association we collaborate with National Health Service and Eye Hospital to organize a programme to distribute spectacles for school service drivers. This programme named as the “vision 2020”.

  • According to the government regulation under the child protection act of Sri Lanka, we as a association had been upgraded our service value by register our service vehicles in child protection authority.

  • In 2011, we started a new registration with the child protection authority along with the respective police stations, for the drivers to get a clearance certificate.

  • Together with City Traffic Unit, All Island School Children Transport Association organized a programme to issue stickers for school vans. This program progress with safety of school children.

  • We proud to inform that as an association we have undergo few interviews and discussions with the chairperson of western province transport authority. The main objectives of these discussions are to develop the private school transport service.

  • In 2010, the collaboration of United Nations development programme, Red Cross and disaster management centre joining hands with all Island School Children transport association, organize a first aid and safety driving program. The ultimate objective of this programme is to protect and maintain the safety of school children. The end of this programme gave more than 1500 first aid boxes to help drivers and school children.

  • In 2009, we had a one day workshop with around 500 members under the guidence of Natural Disaster Management Center at Narahenpitiya which our members were awarded with a participation certificate and a first-aid box.

  • In 2009, we had a one day workshop with around 600 members under the guidence of National child protection authority at Dehiwala which our members were awarded with a participation certificate.

  • In 2008, we had a one day workshop with around 500 members under the guidens of National child protection authority at Kiribathgoda which our members were awarded with a participation certificate.

  • In 2006, we were invited by the “Japan International Cooperation Agency” to study on “Urban Transport Development for Colombo Metropolitan Region in the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka.” which will be implemented up to 2015.
